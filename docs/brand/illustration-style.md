# Monetra Illustration Style

## Direction

Monetra illustrations make financial concepts feel calm, tangible, and
approachable. They use simplified geometric forms, restrained depth, and the
approved green palette.

Illustrations support understanding; they do not compete with financial data.

## Visual Characteristics

- Clean geometric construction with softly rounded corners.
- Simple objects related to everyday finance.
- Flat or lightly dimensional rendering.
- Limited gradients using approved brand colors.
- Soft, purposeful shadows rather than dramatic lighting.
- Generous negative space.
- No text rendered inside illustrations.

## Subject Matter

Preferred subjects include:

- Wallets, cards, receipts, coins, and banknotes.
- Budgets, savings goals, and progress.
- Transaction organization and financial planning.
- Calm empty-state scenes.
- Abstract charts or financial patterns without fake data labels.

Avoid:

- Luxury imagery or promises of instant wealth.
- Fear-based debt imagery.
- Gambling, speculative trading, or cryptocurrency clichés.
- Photorealistic personal financial documents.
- Excessive coins, dollar signs, or decorative money rain.
- Characters expressing shame, panic, or financial judgment.

## Color

Use Monetra Green and Monetra Dark as anchors. Mint, Off White, and neutral
colors provide supporting contrast. Semantic red is reserved for genuine error
or expense meaning and should not dominate an illustration.

## Depth and Lighting

Product illustrations may use subtle depth inspired by the 3D app icon.
Interface illustrations should remain simpler and flatter to reduce visual
noise.

Use a single soft light source. Avoid neon glow, harsh reflections, heavy
glassmorphism, and highly realistic materials.

## Composition

- Keep the subject readable at its intended size.
- Maintain a clear focal point.
- Leave safe space around the subject.
- Use a square composition for empty states and a wide composition for heroes.
- Do not place critical details near crop boundaries.

## Accessibility

Illustrations never carry essential information alone. Provide adjacent text
for the message, action, or status they support.

Decorative illustrations use empty alternative text. Informative illustrations
receive concise alternative text describing their purpose, not every visual
detail.
